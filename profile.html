Gallery Profile

  • 15 - 10 - 1983

    • Abstract rhythms can serve as an expression of the self. Painter Nilesh Baraskar noted this in discussions of his work, as did other abstractionists who sought to move beyond referential notions of reality, focusing instead on the expansive possibilities of an inner world.

    In the development of his work, over the years, Nilesh has moved from watercolor realistic landscapes to more open, spare, and simplified shapes, coupling energetic mark-making with spaciousness and finding his own patterns and textures. He uses acrylic colors to express his vision on canvas. His paintings are engaging in their open-ended exploration of his language, one honed from many years of investigation and excavation.

    Nilesh’s first solo exhibition was held at Pune, India in 2008. He was selected to be a part of fifteen Indian upcoming artists (abstract) showcased in New York in 2009. He has previously been awarded the “Great Art India” award (2006) from Latit Kala Academy for an exhibition at Vishakhapatnam.

    Nilesh is currently working on a new series of paintings titled “Color Clouds”.
